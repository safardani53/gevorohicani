ONE of Jinger Duggar's siblings has shown their support for the star as she slams her family's cult-like behavior in a new podcast interview.

The Counting On alum has been forthcoming regarding her unconventional childhood amid the release of her new tell-all book, Becoming Free Indeed.

Surprisingly, Jinger, 29, has seen some support from her siblings as she addresses her autobiography topics in various interviews.

The reality star recently spoke to Relatable podcast host, Allie Stuckey, about fundamentalism, the extreme sect of Christianity in which she was raised.

The TV personality bashed the leader of the Institute in Basic Life Principles church, Bill Gothard, and his radical teachings which she claimed were harmful in a clipped version on Instagram.

Though Jinger has gone out on a limb to address her troubled childhood in an ultra-conservative household, her sister Joy-Anna, 25, showed a subtle sign of support by "liking" the post.

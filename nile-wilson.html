Nile Wilson | Early Life, Family, and Education

Nile Michael Wilson, the olympian, was born on January 17, 1996. Furthermore, Nile was born in Leeds, West Yorkshire, England, to his parents, Neil and Sally Wilson. 

Talking about his education, Wilson attended Farsley Farfield Primary School for his basic schooling. Likewise, he later completed his high School Education at Pudsey Grangefield School. 

Nile isn’t the only kid in his family. He was raised along with his younger sister Joanna.

Interestingly, his sister is also a gymnastics enthusiast and a national-level player.

Likewise, Nile Wilson also has a celebrity connection in the family. He is the great-nephew of cricket player Charles Duncan Fearnley.

Clumsy as a Kid 

The medal-winning athlete had an interesting childhood with many ups and downs in the early phase of his life. Initially, as a kid, Nile wasn’t much into sports.

He stated that he was a clumsy kid whose parents worried about him and his future.

The clumsiness had led him to many injuries where some of which got imprinted as scars on his body for a lifetime. 

The parents’ worry led them to enroll their child in gymnastics with the intent of helping their kid cope with the clumsiness and be ready for what life has to offer.

This was when all started and marked a remarkable journey for Nile Wilson.

A clumsy kid who developed an interest in gymnastics because he was enrolled to cope with clumsiness later had a great career ahead in the sport.

Isn’t that great to hear only? Indeed it is. It is well said that your childhood shapes your life.

Nile now has prepared himself to overcome any problem in his life as his parents taught him to do so. This event in his childhood has shaped his mind, where nothing can pull him down.

Nile Wilson | Career 

Having spent some early years as a kid who didn’t like any sports, Nile Wilson has come a long way to be in the position he is today in the sports fraternity.

After his parents enrolled him in gymnastics, he slowly developed a great interest in the sport. 

Likewise, Nile caught the eyes of trainers in the gym, who started working with him closely. His trainers put him through several hours of training to get his skills polished for the 2004 Olympics.

However, he, unfortunately, injured himself and had to rest for a while, which put him under stress, and he got depressed.  

Nevertheless, his determination got him back and rolling, and after returning, he won the British Junior Championship in March 2014.

Wilson grabbed the Young Sportsman male honor at the event. It was the first of many awards Nile bagged in his gymnastics career. 

In May, he competed at the 2014 European Championships in Sofia, winning five gold medals. Furthermore, this achievement made Nile the first-ever British gymnast to win five gold medals in the championship.

World Level Competitions 

Nile competed in the 2014 Commonwealth Championship, where he helped the British team bag a gold medal.

Likewise, he bagged 3 individual medals in the competition: a gold in the horizontal bar, a silver in parallel bars, and finally, a bronze one in individual all-round. 

Furthermore, in the 2015 World Championships held in Glasgow, his team came home with a silver medal.

Nile Wilson won a bronze medal in the horizontal bar, making him the first British gymnast to win a bronze medal in the flat bar event in the 2016 Summer Olympics.

To bring upon his 2017 World Championships, Wilson faced an injury on his left ankle ligament while training.

He competed in the 2017 Artistic Gymnastics World Championships but finished in the sixth spot.

The 2018 Commonwealth Games was the most successful championship in his entire career. He won three gold medals: one with the British team and two others in individual all-around and horizontal bars.

However, this was not the end of his medal tally in this championship. He won two silver medals in the rings and parallel bars. 

Nile Wilson | Retirement from Gymnastics

Retirement is certainly an emotional and heartbreaking moment for an athlete. Every athlete wants to be fully dedicated to the sport they love.

But, when you have to retire at an early age due to a circumstance. No one wants that to happen.

However, Nile Wilson’s life suffered a blow when he had to retire from a sport where he achieved great heights due to health problems. Wilson announced his retirement through his Youtube channel.

He uploaded a video titled “I’ve Retired from Gymnastics” to his YouTube channel on January 14, 2021, explaining why he retired from gymnastics. 

 After taking an off from his professional gymnastics, Nike raises his voice against cultural abuse in the arena.

Speaking to BBC Sport, he claimed he was “without a doubt” abused during his training. Further, he was subject to emotional manipulation and was forced to survive in fear of his coaches.

Wilson also told BBC Sport that he waited until retirement to speak against wrongdoings as he was scared of losing his profession, including deselection from the British Olympic team.

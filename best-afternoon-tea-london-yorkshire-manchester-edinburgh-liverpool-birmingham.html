Where's the best afternoon tea in London, Yorkshire, Manchester, Edinburgh, Liverpool and Birmingham

April 21, 2024
blog

AFTERNOON tea is a quintessential British delicacy, and has been exported all around the world.

It was originally a ‘mini-meal’ to tide people over until dinner, but its popularity now sees it enjoyed any time of day.

Around for hundreds of years, it was the pastime of wealthy ladies but became mainstream in the 19th century when Queen Victoria threw tea receptions.

A traditional afternoon tea consists of finger sandwiches, scones with clotted cream and jam and a selection of pastries and cakes served in tiers.

It is accompanied by a pot of tea, and its popularity has seen it spread to nearly every UK city and town.

We’ve rounded some of the best places to get your afternoon tea fix in some of the country’s big cities.

London

Top hotels often offer an afternoon tea package which you can book even if you’re not a guest.

In the capital, some famous spots include Claridge’s which offers a regular price of £60 per person, and a children’s menu for £30.

The Ritz also boasts an afternoon tea, costing £57 per person, and its opulent surroundings are reflected in the dress code – no jeans or trainers allowed.

Covent Garden’s The Savoy is the recipient of an Afternoon Tea Award, winning theirs just last year.

For £60 per person you can enjoy your nibbles accompanied by music from a pianist.

The Sheraton, in Mayfair, also hosts an lavish affair, but is slightly cheaper than its rivals at £39.

It comes highly commended in the Afternoon Tea Awards last year, and you can upgrade your package to include free flowing sparkling wine for £48.

Yorkshire

York also boasts some big hoteliers, with The Hilton serving up a spread for just £21.

And The Grand Hotel and Spa is also reasonably priced, with its tea-time just £25.50, and guests can upgrade to a boozy drink including a Hendrick's Gin and Tonic teapot.

Bettys tea room may not be a famous name but they put on a quality spread, with their afternoon teas just £32.95.

Manchester

The Midland Hotel has a room dedicated to afternoon tea which overlooks St Peter’s Square.

Aptly named the tea room, guests can enjoy a spread from £25.

The Grade II listed Grand Pacific was recently refurbished, and also includes savoury treats such as Cornish crab butter from just £20.

The Radisson Blu hotel serves afternoon tea in its restaurant and terrace from £22.50, and also has a gentleman’s afternoon menu.

Edinburgh

Historic city Edinburgh has a Harvey Nichols tucked away where an afternoon tea can be enjoyed on the top floor of the store, with prices from £22.

Hotel du Vin, a former asylum, offers up afternoon tea with some Scottish twists including smoked salmon.

In the heart of the capital, people can pop in for some refreshments from just £22.50.

If you want some more extravagant surroundings, the 13th century Dalhousie Castle serves up afternoon tea in The Dungeon restaurant.

Guests can enjoy views of the River Esk which sipping on some champagne, with prices from just £18.50.

Liverpool

Harking to its shipbuilding past, enjoy afternoon tea in the Titanic Hotel in the Grade II listed building.

Formerly a warehouse, the spread is served up from £21.

A historic pub dating back to 1870, Ma Boyle’s offers afternoon tea from just £29 in the Victoria-inspired boozer.

Claiming to be the world’s only Beatles-inspired hotel, the Hard Days Night, in the ‘Beatles Quarter’ of the city centre has an afternoon tea menu for just £17.95.

Birmingham

The Plough and Harrow hotel dates back to 1704, and people can relax in its historic setting with an afternoon tea selection costing just £19.50.

What Are The Different Types of Hand Warmers?

As you may already know, there are a variety of hand warmers out there. You will find battery-powered hand warmers, chemical warmers, natural warmers, and more. Some of the most commonly used types are as follows:

Disposable warmers

Disposable warmers are natural hand warmers that use activated charcoal, amongst other materials, to produce heat. These warmers are also called air-activated hand warmers because they require exposure to air to be fully activated. Once activated, they can last several hours. Unfortunately, these hand warmers have to be disposed of after a single use and can not be reused or refilled.

Rechargeable warmers

Battery-powered hand warmers use a rechargeable battery to produce heat within the device. These devices last long enough and can be easily recharged through a power outlet. They also serve as power banks or chargers for other devices such as your mobile phone. Also, you may find different heating modes and sensors in these hand warmers.

Lighter fluid warmers

As the name suggests, lighter fluid hand warmers use lighter fluid and a flame to heat up. This fluid is securely packed in the hand warmer and continues to provide heat to you until the fuel runs out. These hand warmers can be a great alternative to rechargeable warmers as you may not have access to electric outlets everywhere. That being said, you must be careful when carrying a lighter fluid hand warmer around.

Are Hand Warmers Safe To Use?

Considering how heating is the primary function of warmers, it is important to ensure that they are safe for use. Generally, hand warmers are regarded as safe to use. They may feel slightly uncomfortable when you first use them but your skin soon eases into the warmth. But, if you’re using a quick-heating hand warmer, you may want to look out for burns, especially if you have sensitive skin. But, overall, they don’t cause any harm to your skin as long as you use them carefully.

How To Buy a Hand Warmer

Types of hand warmer

The type of hand warmer refers to its heat source. If the heat source is electric, you’re using a rechargeable electric warmer, whereas if the heat source is chemical or fluid you’re using a natural or refillable hand warmer.

Having already discussed the types of hand warmers, you just need to figure out which type suits you the most. If you’re going to be in a remote area with no likelihood of electrical outlets, for example, your best bet is to go for disposable or lighter fluid warmers.

Longevity

The best kind of hand warmer is the one that remains heated for a long time. Most hand warmers last for a couple of hours, while others may even last up to 10 hours. It’s always a good idea to go for a warmer that lasts longer.

Weight

Hand warmers are supposed to fit in your hand so they can easily keep it warm for you. As a result, all hand warmers are compact. That being said, some might be lightweight while others can be bulky. You should avoid bulky hand warmers if you’re going to be doing any outdoor activities. The lighter your hand warmer is, the easier carrying it around will be.

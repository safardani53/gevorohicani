Bonanza for Scrabble fans as 300 new words are added to its official dictionary

April 26, 2024
blog

IT is a red letter day for Scrabble fans as 300 new words have been added to its official dictionary.

And it is not just obscure terms allowed in – the long-awaited validation of ‘OK’ has been revealed.

Scrabble fans can rejoice after 300 new worlds have been added to its official dictionaryNew word “bizjet”, a small business plane, is worth up to 120 points as an opening play.

Merriam-Webster has released the latest and sixth edition of The Official Scrabble Players Dictionary, four years after the last revamp.

The company published the first official Scrabble dictionary in 1976. Until then, the games rules allowed any dictionary to be consulted by players.

Here, Ben Leo picks out more of the latest additions to help you score.

Twerking could earn Miley Cyrus 12 points next time she's reminiscing with Robin Thicke over a game of ScrabbleThe official version of the dictionary is now updated every four to eight years.

Peter Sokolowski, editor at large at Merriam-Webster said: “OK is something Scrabble players have been waiting for, for a long time.

“Basically two and three-letter words are the lifeblood of the game.”

The Merriam-Webster version is typically used by American players of the board game, with UK publisher Collins putting out a Brit-focused dictionary. This has not been updated since 2015.

The new Scrabble words

ARANCINI – Deep-fried risotto balls.

AQUAFABA – Water which pulses or seeds have been cooked in.

BEATDOWN – A violent physical beating.

BIBIMBAP – Korean rice dish.

BOKEH – Out-of-focus area of a picture.

BOTNET – Network of computers infected with viruses.

COTIJA –Hard dry Mexican cheese.

EMOJI – Digital cartoon faces, pictures and symbols.

EW – To express disgust or taste.

FACEPALM – Act of putting head in one’s palm out of frustration.

FROWNY – To contract the eyebrows as an expression of disapproval.

HIVEMIND – Group thinking.

MACARON – Small round cake.

NUBBER – In baseball, a batted ball that does not travel very far.

OK – Affirmation. Yes or understood.

PUGGLE – Cross-breed of Beagle and Pug dogs.

QAPIK – Azerbaijan currency unit.

SCHNEID – German phrase for sports’ team losing streak.

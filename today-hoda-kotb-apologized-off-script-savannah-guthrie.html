TODAY show’s Hoda Kotb has apologized to her co-hosts after going a little off script during Friday’s broadcast.

The 58-year-old’s actions have caused Savannah Guthrie to wonder if there was a “memo” about it all.

During Friday’s show, hosts Hoda, Savannah, and Craig Melvin all kept to a similar color scheme for their attire with variations of grays and blues.

Though they all wore darker clothing, Hoda had someone else on set whose outfit seemed to match hers exactly.

While chatting with NBC News correspondent Kristen Dahlgren about different shopping events and deals, the TV personalities noticed that they were twinning.

Both Hoda and Kristen had on silky blue button-ups and black slacks.

The realization that they were matching caused the anchor to go a little off script, get up from the desk, and head over to stand side by side with Kristen.

“I’m sorry,” Hoda said as she moved away from the anchor desk and started her way over to the correspondent.

Savannah, who wore black pants like the others but had on a purple-and-black animal print top, asked them: “Was there, like, a memo, or?”

At first, Hoda just thought she and the correspondent had on the same top, but then she discovered their entire outfit matched once they were standing next to one another.

The pair gave each other a double high five in excitement, before Hoda made her way back to her chair next to Savannah.

The day before, Hoda sat right next to Craig, instead, as Savannah was missing from the show.

However, she called in to explain her on-set absence.

She had traveled to Washington, D.C. on Wednesday to receive the 2022 Caregiver Champion award at the Elizabeth Dole Foundation Heroes and History Makers gala that night.

Savannah then called into the studio from the capital on Thursday to talk to her co-workers about the honor.

"Savannah, what a night. That looked beautiful. We are so proud of you," Hoda said, as she and Craig clapped.

The host thanked them and told her co-hosts that the gala raised a lot of money for a worthy cause.

Both TJ and Ferson agreed to the judge's order that neither will do any "threatening, injuring maltreating, vilifying, molesting or harassing the adverse party," as a result of the divorce.

Meanwhile, a legal expert has claimed that TJ's alleged affairs can impact his custody case if a court finds that the person's extramarital relationship impacted their ability to parent.

Lisa Zeiderman, a New York divorce attorney and partner at Miller Zeiderman, said that one of the questions that might be examined in a custody case such as TJ's - which came with so much press coverage - is examining the impact a high-profile alleged affair could have on a child.

"This lack of privacy, and the degree to which a parent has contributed to that lack of privacy, I don't think would be helpful in a custody case."

The expert expanded on how an affair may impact a child, such as Sabine, who recently celebrated her 10th birthday.

Lisa said some of the questions that could come up in court could include: "How did that relationship affect their ability to parent?

"Did they make choices to be with the person they have these extramarital affairs with at times that they could have been with their children?"

In addition to that, the court will also look at the bottom line, if money was spent on an alleged affair that should have been earmarked for one's family.

"There is also the question of equitable distribution, or what we call a wasteful dissipation of assets," Lisa added.

"The question becomes: Because of your affair were you wastefully dissipating assets?

"Were you paying for things, gifts, extravagant luxury things, and that kind of stuff, and does that need to go back into the marital pie?"

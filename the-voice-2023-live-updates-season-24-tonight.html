What to know about finalist Ruby Leigh

Hailing from Foley, Missouri, Ruby Leigh made her TV debut on The Voice's 24th season.

During the show's round of blind auditions, Ruby performed a cover of Patsy Montana's I Want To Be a Cowboy's Sweetheart.

Although all four judges turned the chair around for her, she chose to be a part of Reba McEntire's team.

Both the coaches and the viewers have often pointed out Ruby's distinct yodeling abilities as a signature of her singing style.

Jordan Rainer claps back at her fans

Team Reba's Jordan Rainer, who was sent home last night, shared a message to her critics on Instagram following her elimination from the competition.

"Regarding the people who’ve felt the compulsion to message me and comment their elation that I’m gone… who’ve felt the need to point to my 'instant save song' last night as proof I never belonged on this show… I’m going to say this once. Because I need to," the singer captioned her post of a black and white photo of herself.

"I know I bombed the instant save song. Any singer who’s done this for more than 5 minutes knows when they’re bombing. It’s the most sickening feeling—like a roller coaster thats falling apart while you’re on it, and you can’t get off of it."

The Top 9 contestant continued, "Folks— I had the pressure of the world on my shoulders; the fear and dread of 'losing'; my whole family in the live audience who flew out to support me and my crushing desire to make them proud; the desire to make my coach proud. The desire to make my Chickasaw tribe and my state of Oklahoma proud.

"As a perfectionist…as someone who tries harder than ANYONE WILL EVER KNOW to nail everything I do… having to feel like I failed in front of everyone is hard enough without people pointing at my hardest performance as proof I never belonged here; as 'proof' I can’t sing; using it to gloat and say 'finally she’s gone'…

"So please… y’all who are messaging and emailing me such evil and deflating things… stop," the singer wrote.

Nini Iris shares message after her elimination

Top 9 contestant Nini Iris took to Instagram after being eliminated from the show Tuesday night to share a video message for her fans.

"Hey beautiful people," the Georgian singer said in the post.

"I wanted to thank each and every one of you for voting for me. I'm extremely happy with the way things turned out, even though I'm not gonna be in the finale."

She continued, "It's okay because I think I've done such a great job on this show and I put out such quality performances on this show that I'm always gonna cherish, and I'm always gonna remember.

"I just can't wait for what life has to bring me. I can't wait to put out some of the covers that I've done on this show.

"I can't wait to put out my original songs and I can't wait to see you at my shows because there's gonna be a lot of them and I'm so excited for everything that the future holds.

"I love you so much. Please be kind to one another, be kind to the people that made it through. I love them so much and I root for every one of them. I love you and I'll see you so soon," she concluded.

MICHY BATSHUAYI welcomed Kepa Arrizabalaga to the “memes club” after Chelsea’s goalie scored an embarrassing own goal with his FACE against Ajax.

Frank Lampard’s men were 4-1 down to the Dutch side at Stamford Bridge last night.

But they fought back for a 4-4 draw before bagging a winner which was then controversially ruled out by VAR.

The number two was the order of the day in West London, with Jorginho netting two penalties, Ajax having two men sent off and Chelsea scoring two own goals.

The first came after just two minutes when Tammy Abraham accidentally turned Quincy Promes’ free-kick into his own net.

But the second came courtesy of goalie Kepa, who saw Hakim Ziyech’s free-kick sail past him to hit the inside of the post, only to rebound straight into his face and trickle into the goal.

Kepa, 25, was mercilessly mocked on social media after the hilarious incident.

And teammate Batshuayi has now joined in with the jests and japes.

The Belgian striker, 26, set the World Cup alight in 2018 when he brilliantly smashed a football into a post while celebrating his country’s only goal in the group stage win over England.

But the ball didn’t go very far, rebounding straight back into Batshuayi’s face and leaving the forward in a lot of pain.

Batshuayi quickly took to Twitter to console Kepa, but his offerings read more like mockery.

Batshuayi wrote: “Welcome to the memes club bro (handshake emoji) (bandaged face emoji).”

This prompted a response from former teammate Cesc Fabregas, now at Monaco.

He simply replied with two crying-with-laughter face emojis, no doubt enjoying the baiting.

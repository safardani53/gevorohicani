Tony Jefferson | Career

Before stepping on the professional line, Tony was a boxer and was close to being on the 1992 U.S. Olympic team.

As a young child, he boxed from 11 to 15 years old.

He earned the nickname “Cobra” during that time due to his fast hands and swift movements.

According to the sources, Tony remained undefeated in his local tournaments. 

Later, he started to play football and even dropped out of it later to focus on boxing.

But after he missed football, he was back at it again. 

The NFL Arena  

In 2013, Tony Jefferson started to forgo his senior collegiate year and join the 2013 NFL Draft.

ESPN ranked him as the fifth-best safety prospect in the draft. However, his injuries slowed him down during the draft. 

Altogether, Tony Jefferson got undrafted in 2013, yet the Arizona Cardinals signed him on a three-year contract.

He joined the training camp with them, where he stood as the strong backup safety behind Yeremiah Bell. 

Later, he had his professional regular season debut against the St. Louis Rams.

Following it, he also played as the starting free safety before being the backup free safety. It continued up to 2016 until he re-signed his contract with the team. 

It was a year deal with a right of first refusal clause, and he played for them until he became an unrestricted free agent for the Baltimore Ravens.

Playing for Baltimore Ravens, he became the second-best FREE-AGENT safety.

He signed a four-year contract with the Ravens and stayed with them till 2020.

Following it, Tony signed a year deal with the San Francisco 49ers on June 2021 but ended up on the practice squad. 

Tony re-signed with the Baltimore Ravens on December 2021 and e stayed with them until August 2022. 

On September 2022, Tony signed a 1-year contract with the New York Giants. 

Injuries

Throughout his professional career days, Jefferson has faced several injuries.

One of his earliest injuries dates back to 2013. He had a hamstring injury that affected his drilling and running ability back then. 

Following it, Tony faced a concussion in October 2014. Not long after, he suffered a knee sprain in December 2016 during the season’s penultimate game. 

In 2018, Tony struggled with a pedal ankle sprain that required surgery. The following year, an ACL tear got him out of the remaining season. 

In October 2022, Tony suffered a foot injury and was placed on injured reserve. According to the New York Giants reporters, on November 28, 2022, Tony returned to practice.

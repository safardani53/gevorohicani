Costco brings back popular $8.99 bakery item fans are saying they ate the whole box in a week

A POPULAR bakery item has returned to Costco and fans are gobbling away at it.

As seasons come and go, retailers bring new items to the shelves, and Costco is no exception.

And some that have been there in the past will make a comeback.

But Costco has great news for fans of the mini lemon cakes, as a local store employee confirmed to The U.S. Sun that they are now back on shelves.

They were spotted by the fan account @costcohotfinds.

The page is run by a mom who calls herself a "Costco addict."

In a video, she showed off the mini lemon cakes, which is muffin base topped with icing and what appears to be sprinkles.

The ingredients were unclear, but most customers in the comment section loved them.

One fan said: "We just got these and holy cow they are amazing!!!"

Another positive comment read: "Bought some this weekend! Couldn’t stop gobbling it up haha."

And a third wrote: “I LOVED THESE, I ate the whole box in a week all by myself."

While prices can vary by location, the mini cakes were found in six-packs by costcohotfinds for $8.99.

It's unclear if they will only be available for a limited time in stores.

OTHER COSTCO BAKERY FINDS

But the mini lemon cakes are far from the only item making news in Costco's bakery section.

The warehouse chain has launched four new sweet items, which are as follows:

  • Strawberries & cream bar cake
  • Sour cream butter pound cake
  • Key lime pie
  • S'mores cookies

The cheapest item on the list is the pound cake for $7.99, which comes with both sour cream batter and icing.

The most expensive item is the Strawberries & cream bar cake at $17.99.

It is made with a sponge cake, strawberry filling, and is topped with whipped cream.

NEW SUMMER ITEMS

This includes The Coffee Bean & Tea Leaf, which launched new budget-friendly offerings.

The Pepsi Pineapple can be ordered at one of its restaurants in 16 ounces with a large thin-crust two-topping pizza for $9.99 online, starting today.

McDonald's fans have spotted the Cookies & Crème Pie in the US.

While prices can vary by location, the sweet treat contains 220 calories and sells for $1.99.

McDonald's told The U.S. Sun that it will only be available in participating markets including Los Angeles, Seattle, Chicago, and New York.

Tim Horton's has released four new drinks that start at $3.50.

This includes the Creamy Coconut Iced Capp, White Chocolate Macadamia Flavored Drinks, and two drinks with a watermelon twist.

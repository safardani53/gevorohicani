Tragic cancer boy's mum pays tribute to 'murdered' Sheffield teen who shaved his head into a mohawk

May 16, 2024
blog

THE mum of a tragic cancer boy today paid tribute to a teen who raised cash for her sick son before he was allegedly murdered.

Tristan Barrass dyed his hair yellow and shaved it into a mohawk to raise funds for pal Kasabian Newton-Smith, who was just eight years old when he died in 2016.

Tragically, Tristan, 13, and his 14-year-old brother Blake died in Shiregreen, Sheffield, on Friday with their mum Sarah Barrass now charged with two counts of murder.

Today, the teen boy was remembered for having a “good heart” by Kasabian’s mum Kay Newton-Smith.

Speaking to the Sun Online, the mum said she had at first met the Barrass family during a charity fundraiser in 2010 for a new neuro ward at Sheffield Children’s Hospital.

And Kay revealed how she was struck by Tristan’s caring nature.

She said: “Sarah once shaved her head to support my son who had cancer.

“Her son Tristan also shaved his head for Kas. He had no hair because of his illness and treatment and Tristan wanted to show him that he looked the same as him and so he could fit in.

“Another time Tristan had a yellow Mohawk to support Kas. My son loved yellow, it’s a bright, happy colour.”

Kay said she had been left shocked when she found out about the boys' deaths and Sarah's arrest.

After news broke in the community, she took to social media, saying: “Ok here’s a long shot instead of spreading rumours and negativity let’s spread this light of love for the family of shire green and all affected”.

She included a photograph of candles set in a love heart shape.

A COMMUNITY IN MOURNING

Kay’s son Kasabian had been inundated with support during his fight against the disease, including from young Tristan.

Kasabian had been diagnosed with Ewing sarcoma and gone through more than 100 radiation treatments in his battle against cancer.

In December 2016, his family announced he had “passed peacefully in his sleep” - just five days before his ninth birthday.

And as a show of support for the sick student, Tristan dyed his own hair yellow, telling BBC show Our School: “My friend is dying of bone cancer, I asked if I could dye my hair yellow for him.

“His favourite colour is yellow.”

He added that his hair “makes me who I want to be. With normal hair, you look like any other person”.

MUM IN COURT

Mum Sarah Barrass, 34, is expected to go on trial later this year accused of killing her two teen sons.

Her half-brother Brandon Machin, 38, has also been accused of murdering his nephews.

Both were remanded in custody at Sheffield Crown Court over the tragedy. Police were scrambled to the home in the Shiregreen area of the city over concerns for the occupants’ welfare about 7.30am on Friday.

Horrified neighbours told how one boy was taken away on a stretcher as paramedics frantically tried to resuscitate him.

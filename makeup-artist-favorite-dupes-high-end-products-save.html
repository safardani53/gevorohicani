I'm a makeup artist - these are my favorite dupes for high-end products to save you over $30

September 09, 2024
blog

YOU don't have to sacrifice quality to save money on beauty products.

Rather than splurging on new makeup, you can buy affordable dupes.

Beauty gurus have found dupes for even the most prestigious products, like the Charlotte Tilbury Hollywood Flawless Filter foundation.

These dupes are a fraction of the price but still offer the benefits of their high-end counterparts.

Rocio Soria is a makeup artist sharing her favorite dupes on her TikTok, @Rocio.Roses.

She showed how perfectly some of her dupes match up by doing half of her face in the high-end products and the other in the dupes.

1. Lancôme Teint Idole Foundation

Rocio worked as a Lancôme sales rep, so she's very familiar with this product.

This foundation promises buildable coverage and a soft glow finish, plus 40 shades to choose from.

However, at $47 a bottle, it is on the pricier side.

Rocio recommends the Catrice Cosmetics HD Liquid Coverage foundation.

Both products are oil-free and offer 24-hour wear, but the Catrice Cosmetics foundation is only $11.

2. Rare Beauty Liquid Blush

Selena Gomez took the beauty world by storm when she released her makeup brand called Rare Beauty.

The Soft Pinch Liquid Blush quickly became a fan favorite, retailing for $20.

Rocio was able to find a dupe for less than $5.

The Profusion Cosmetics Blush Hour Liquid Blush in the shade Paloma retails for $4.98 and is a perfect dupe for Rare Beauty's Grateful.

3. Anastasia Beverly Hills Iced Out Highlighter

This highlighter is a cult favorite among beauty fans.

Rather than spending $34, Rocio suggests getting the $6.49 Wet N Wild Care Bears Collection Highlight in the shade Let Your Light Shine.

It offers the same shine and finish as the Iced Out Highlighter.

How to find dupes

Dancing with the Stars performer and TikTok user Linsday Arnold, who goes by lindsarnold on the platform, posted a video explaining her strategy for finding cheap alternatives to name-brand shoes and clothing.

To take advantage of this method, you'll need to shop through the Amazon app on a smartphone.

The app lets you search by images in addition to text by clicking the square icon in the search bar, and you can upload any image in your camera roll and see similar products.

Lindsay screenshotted a pair of $1,045 pumps from the high-end brand Amina Muaddi and used the picture to run an Amazon search.

The app then presented her with tons of similar shoes - except they were $1,000 cheaper.

She settled on a nearly identical $55 pair, while other dupes were listed for $42.

Lindsay also pointed out that you can upload photos of full outfits and toggle between search results for each individual article of clothing.

For instance, she used a screenshot of a model wearing a STAUD top, and she was shown dupes for the top and the model's jeans.

And while she only used this feature to find dupes of clothes and accessories, you can search by any image on Amazon.

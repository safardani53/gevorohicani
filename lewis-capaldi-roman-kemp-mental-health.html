AROUND 90,000 fans will be descending on Wembley Stadium tomorrow for the biggest summer party of the year.

But despite flying high at the top of the charts, with the fastest-selling album of the year, Lewis Capaldi will not be on the line-up of huge names.

He surprised fans this week by announcing he is stepping back from the show and all other commitments for three weeks, admitting he was struggling to cope.

And Summertime Ball host Roman Kemp, a close pal of the Scottish singer, says he couldn’t be prouder of his mate for speaking out despite the disappointment he won’t be able to party on the night.

Roman, who hosts Capital FM’s weekday breakfast show, tells me: “Of course I’m gutted we won’t see him — but actually I’m really proud of him for speaking out.

“It’s so important people realise it’s OK to take time out and look after yourself and your mental wellbeing — so somebody like Lewis who is so honest and open with fans can make a real difference.

“I sent him a message wishing him well and hopefully he’ll feel back to his best again soon.

"But it’s so brave to speak out, especially when you are at the very top of your game like he is.

“There’s an assumption that if you’re very successful and things are going well you can’t struggle with mental health, but that just isn’t true.

"He’s setting a great example to people and I wish him all the best.”

Despite Lewis pulling out, the line-up is still packed with star names including headliners Calvin Harris, Jess Glynne, Niall Horan, The Jonas Brothers and Ellie Goulding.

But Roman has a trick up his sleeve to get himself noticed among all the A-listers — by climbing on to the roof of the vast stadium to open the show.

He says with a laugh: “The thing is, we always try and have a bit of a stunt to get things started, and this sounded like a great idea.

"But we haven’t worked out how we’re going to get me down afterwards.

“I asked about doing like a bungee thing down to the stage, but let’s be honest — I don’t want to mess my hair up.

“It’ll be a challenge, but I’m excited. How can you not be excited by Wembley Stadium?

“I’ve not made it as a footballer, so this is as close as I’m going to get.”

This year’s event will be broadcast on ITV1 for the first time, airing on Saturday, June 17 in a highlights package fronted by Roman.

And he’s vowed to savour every second after being advised by dad Martin Kemp, whose band Spandau Ballet performed at the venue during Live Aid but didn’t quite appreciate it at the time.

Despite the huge party, Roman admits he won’t be sticking around to party long into the night after struggling to get up for his early morning show, which he presents alongside co-hosts Sian Welby and Chris Stark.

He adds: “I’ve learnt my lesson. Sian is the party animal usually — I’ve seen her host a show with a plastic bucket next to her microphone — and Chris hasn’t done this before so I reckon he might get carried away.

“I’ll stick around and hang out but I know when to call it quits these days. As Ed Sheeran sings, ‘Nothing happens after two’.”

The star, who also regularly presents The One Show on BBC1, will be spending today’s build-up at a doctor’s clinic after damaging his knee, in the hope that painkilling injections will help him get through the show.

But it won’t stop him dancing along to the biggest names on the night as he backs Adam Lambert to blow the roof off the stadium by performing Queen hits and Calvin to close the show in style.

Roman explains: “There’s something about the idea of Queen songs at Wembley that you can’t help getting excited by.

“And Calvin is just a megastar with so many hits. It’s going to be a banging show.”

Last year Roman’s pal Harry Styles headlined — but the radio DJ barely recognised him when he recently bumped into the former One Direction heart-throb at a London gym because he was looking so buff.

He revealed: “I saw him from behind, and it was only the tattoos that gave it away.

“He’s really muscled up. He looks good for it, but he joked that he looks like he’s joined Geordie Shore.

“I’m sure his fans won’t complain.”

Along with Tommy, Joel Corry is also making his debut at the game, playing for England.

Tom said: “Bugzy is a machine, he is a big defender.”

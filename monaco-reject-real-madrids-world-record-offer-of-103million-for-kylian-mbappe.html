Monaco reject Real Madrid's world record offer of 103million to bring in star striker Kylian Mbapp

July 24, 2024
blog

REAL MADRID have sensationally had a world record bid of £103million turned down by Monaco for their boy wonder Kylian Mbappe.

Leonardo Jardim's side are desperate to keep hold of the 18-year-old sensation who has lit up Europe with his dazzling performances this season.

According to the Telegraph, Los Blancos submitted the offer in the last few days, but the Ligue 1 side have turned down the offer and are instead desperate to tie him down with a lucrative new deal.

The fee would eclipse the £89million Manchester United paid Juventus for Paul Pogba, but no deal has so far been struck, with the French club aiming to hold off until at least 2018 after the World Cup in Russia.

However, the Les Bleus international is believed to be keen on a switch to join the illustrious squad of Zinedine Zidane.

Monaco CEO and vice-president said: "The plan is to do everything to prolong him (Mbappe) and to do everything that he stays a minimum one more year with us."

Speaking about whether they would reject any offers for him, he added: "We will, although that will depend, of course, also on the player.

"We will make an offer to prolong his contract and hopefully he will accept and stay with us. I think in the month of June, before the season starts.

"He will definitely be one of the top players in the world. He has everything to succeed.

Mbappe fired Monaco to clinch a historic league championship for the first time in 17 years, where his 26 goals in all competitions also guided his side to the Champions League semi-finals.

The teenager revealed to Telefoot he will announce his decision to either carry on playing in the principality or make a summer switch after the international fixtures with France.

He told Telefoot: "My future? For the moment I do not know.

"We were targeting the title and we left everything else aside because I wanted to win a trophy with the club I have spent my life with.

"I put all rumours and questions about my future to the back of my mind and I will reveal it after the games I have with France."

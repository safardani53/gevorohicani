How much do French bulldogs cost?

August 12, 2024
blog

THE French bulldog is one of the most popular breeds around.

They are small in size, fun-loving pets, happy to live in apartments or houses - but how much will buying one set you back?

What is a French Bulldog?

They are the result of a cross between miniature bulldogs from England and terrier types of dogs in Paris, France, in the 1800s.

It is their bat-like ears that sets them apart form the larger bulldog breed.

Their smaller size means they weigh under 28 pounds and stand at a height of approximately 11-13 inches.

They have solid bodies and a large head with wrinkles around a short nose.

The American Kennel Club ranks them fourth in popularity out of 197 breeds.

They are great companions and very adaptable.

How much do French Bulldogs cost?

On average French bulldogs can go $1,500-$3,000 but top-quality dogs with outstanding breed lines can cost between $5,500 and $10,000.

Their high resale value due to their popularity makes them a target for thieves - and their small size makes them easier to grab.

Why are French bulldogs so expensive?

The unique Frenchies are expensive for a number of reasons, mainly because their breeding can be costly.

French bulldogs find it difficult to mate naturally due to the females slim hips, so they generally require artificial insemination and C-sections to reproduce.

High-quality foods, vet bills and housing only add to the cost of breeding Frenchies.

Why do French bulldogs cry so much?

French bulldogs aren't known for barking a lot, they do however express their feelings in other ways, including by crying.

That means your Frenchie might be trying to tell you, that it's hungry, thirsty, cold or sick.

The most common reason behind their crying though is that they often suffer from separation anxiety.

French bulldogs do not like to be left alone - they do have a long history accompanying people around towns.

What is the lifespan of a French bulldog?

The life expectancy of a French bulldog is between 10 and 14 years.

However, owners need to look out for health issues such as breathing problems.

They may also suffer from back problems, cataracts and kneecaps slipping out of place.

While they might be alright for short periods of time, French bulldogs do not deal well with loneliness.

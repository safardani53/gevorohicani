Happy Halloween, Barbie!

If you’ve been dreaming about which pink ensemble you’ll try to recreate this October after watching Greta Gerwig’s Barbie, then you’re not alone — in fact, we’ve gone ahead and found the best costumes for you in advance. Whether you’re going for Margot Robbie’s fuschia western ensemble from her first real-world costume change or simply want to go all-pink in honor of the Barbiecore trend, we scoured the internet to find the best outfits to pay tribute to this American icon. (Don’t worry, we have options that bring the Kenergy, too.)

Barbie’s fashion influence has spanned decades of style, which makes these costumes pretty timeless — and is exactly what Oscar-winning costume designer, Jacqueline Durran, was going for. “I start off with 1959, obviously, then there’s the beach, which is very much influenced by the early ‘60s,” she previously told PEOPLE. “I tried to set up this idea that we were going through time, but at the same time, I wanted the passing of time to be related to the past of Mattel.”

Though the cast’s bubblegum pink wardrobe is studded with Chanel, you can recreate these looks for a lot less by leaning into whichever decade you choose. Durran explains wanting to encapsulate the 60-plus years of Barbie’s fashion without lending the costumes too heavily to a certain period, so they could evoke nostalgia in fans of every age. “That was the intention — that it would touch different people's memories at different times," she said. "It's timeless.”

Whether your plans involve a soirée at the Dreamhouse or you end up stuck at a Mojo Dojo Casa House party, we found the best Barbie-inspired Halloween costumes that will have you ready to “dance the night” away.

How to Pick the Right Barbie Halloween Costume

Recreation vs. Original

The first place to start when deciding which kind of Barbie you want to channel this year is thinking about which looks stand out to you. If there are any particular moments from the film you love, like Robbie’s ‘60s gingham beach dress and seashell necklace, for example, then you can recreate the look with this nearly identical look on Amazon for a whole lot less. But if you simply want to put together a Barbie-inspired look, you can opt for pieces like this bubblegum pink logo-embossed mini dress, some Barbie-inspired shoes, and a magenta dumpling purse to become your very own Barbie.

Costume Pieces

Shopping for Halloween costumes can get pricey pretty quickly if you don’t already own pieces of your ‘fit, which is why it’s sometimes best to go for an all-inclusive costume. Spirit Halloween has several replicas of the movie’s costumes ready for October 2023, which make it easy to complete your look with just one purchase instead of having to hunt down several separate pieces — which can be expensive in both time and money. 

The rollerblading Ken costume, for example, comes with everything you need (besides the rollerblades), whereas the Barbie cardigan is sold alone, meaning you’ll have to piece the rest of the costume together by yourself. But if you’re going for a “custom” Barbie look, then you may love this personalized take despite a bit more effort it entails.

Price

Getting glammed up (or not) is entirely up to each individual as they search for the perfect Halloween costume, but it’s often an endeavor that can get expensive rather quickly. It can sometimes be more budget-friendly to get a costume that comes with all the pieces together, like some of our picks from Spirit Halloween, rather than buying piecemeal garments here and there to create an outfit by the holiday.

If you are planning on utilizing pieces that already exist in your current wardrobe, then it may be more advantageous to buy one or two on-theme pieces over an entirely new look. Decide on which Barbie you want to be first, and take it from there.
